Chronological Snobbery /ˌkrɒnəˈlɒdʒɪkəl ˈsnɒbəri/

The uncritical acceptance of the intellectual climate common to our own age and the assumption that whatever has gone out of date is on that account discredited. Coined by C.S. Lewis in collaboration with Owen Barfield.
